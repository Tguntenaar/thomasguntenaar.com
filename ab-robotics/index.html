TG / Robotics Part 1 2026 ⟶
— 01

We're at the early precipice of a nonlinear transformation in industrial society. Automation is undergoing a revolution that enables full-scale automation of all manufacturing.

— 02

These systems are the first industrial input that is not supplemental but fully additive — 24/7 labor with higher throughput than any human. Not one more unit of work. A new axis of it.

— 03

In robotics, the impact compounds. Robotics systems manufacturing more robotics systems — each unit drives cost down and quality up, strengthening the flywheel. Ad infinitum.

— 04

Robotics is a general-purpose technology. Its gains spill horizontally across every sector — textiles, electronics, consumer goods, automotive — not one industry, all of them.

— 05

Should China unlock this first, it will iterate faster, subsidize deeper, and oversupply every market. The general-purpose robotics boom becomes an existential threat to a West that is outcompeted in all capacities.

/ 02 — China's Playbook
Batteries → solar → EVs → robots

The same three moves, run again.

— Move 01
SCALE
Iterate fastest

In Shenzhen, DJI could source any part from any factory within hours and iterate at an unreal speed. GoPro's Karma shipped from three countries and took weeks per loop. By 2016 DJI had already won — 80% of the global commercial drone market.

— Move 02
FLOOD
Oversupply the market

Before a Chinese competitor ever enters the West, it has already won on cost. Then production capacity floods in, prices are cut aggressively, and every incumbent is undercut into "margin challenges." It's a competition of scale — decided before it starts.

— Move 03
∞
Spin the flywheel

Robots build robots. KUKA's Guangdong plant already builds one robot every half hour, aiming for one a minute. Each unit lowers cost and raises quality, which sells more units. The loop repeats ad infinitum — and the door closes behind it.

/ 03 — The Holy Grail

A robot that does any task, any environment.

— Stage 01
Structured

Today's robots: rigid, fragile, predefined. One task, static environment. Any deviation breaks the process — the constraint that stifled the industry for generations.

— Stage 02
Partially unstructured

The first movement out of the cell. Robots handle more than one task inside the factory, sliding across the spectrum until they can automate every step.

— Stage 03
Human-populated

The hardest domain: dynamic, unpredictable, shared with people. Foundation models, simulated data and cheap electric actuators have finally cracked the door.

— Stage 04
General purpose

Any task, any environment — the factory becomes one living organism. Whoever crosses first captures nearly all labor demand, in perpetuity.
